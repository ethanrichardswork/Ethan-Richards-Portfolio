      <div id="info-text">
         Here is a timeline of Mandela's life:
         </div>
      </figure>
      </p>
        <div id="timeline-info">
          <ul>
            <li>
              <strong>1918</strong>
              - Born in Mvezo, South Africa.
            </li>
            <li>
              <strong>1939</strong>
              - Enrolls at the University of Fort Hare, the only higher education institute for Black South Africans at the time.
            </li>
            <li><strong>1940</strong>
             - Expelled from Fort Hare for participating in a student protest.
            </li>
            <li>
              <strong>1941</strong>
            - Moves to Johannesburg and works as a night watchman before joining a law firm as a clerk.
            </li>
            <li>
              <strong>1943</strong>
              - Completes his BA degree through the University of South Africa and enrolls at the University of Witwatersrand for a law degree.
            </li>
            <li>
              <strong>1944</strong>
              - Joins the African National Congress (ANC) and co-founds the ANC Youth League. Marries Evelyn Mase.
            </li>
            <li>
              <strong>1952</strong>
              - Opens the first Black law firm in South Africa with Oliver Tambo, offering legal services to those affected by apartheid laws. Launches the Defiance Campaign, a non-violent resistance against apartheid.
            </li>
            <li>
              <strong>1956</strong>
              - Arrested along with 155 activists and charged with treason. The trial lasts five years before all are acquitted. 
            </li>
            <li>
              <strong>1960</strong>
              - The Sharpeville Massacre occurs, leading to the banning of the ANC. Mandela goes underground to continue the fight against apartheid.
            </li>
            <li>
              <strong>1961</strong>
              - Co-founds the armed wing of the ANC, Umkhonto we Sizwe, in response to government violence.
            </li>
            <li>
              <strong>1962</strong>
              - Captured and sentenced to five years in prison for incitement and leaving the country without permission.
            </li>
            <li>
              <strong>1964</strong>
              - Given a life sentence at the Rivonia Trial for sabotage and conspiracy to overthrow the government. Sent to Robben Island Prison.
            </li>
            <li>
              <strong>1982</strong>
              - Transferred to Pollsmoor Prison after 18 years on Robben Island.
            </li>
            <li>
              <strong>1988</strong>
              - Moved to Victor Verster Prison.
            </li>
            <li>
              <strong>1990</strong>
              - Released from prison after 27 years. The ANC is unbanned, and negotiations to end apartheid begin.
              </li>
              <li>
                <strong>1993</strong>
                - Awarded the Nobel Peace Prize alongside President F.W. de Klerk for their efforts in dismantling apartheid.
              </li>
              <li>
                <strong>1994</strong>
                - Becomes South Africa's first Black president after the country's first democratic elections.
              </li>
              <li>
                <strong>1999</strong>
                - Steps down as president after one term, focusing on humanitarian work.
              </li>
              <li>
                <strong>2004</strong>
                - Officially retires from public life but continues advocacy work.
              </li>
              <li>
                <strong>2013</strong>
                - Passes away at the age of 95.
              </li>
        </div>
